External Student Assessments

Gyan Shala adapts best global practices from various education experts to the local context, ensuring high-quality and effective education through a dedicated team of around 1,500 teachers, field supervisors and designers cum managers.

We prove that geography doesn't define potential by bringing best classroom practices such as whole-class teaching, peer learning via group activities in small groups, and specialized workbooks for daily self-learning into urban slum settings, we maintain classroom standards that rival elite institutions.

To ensure we stay on track and improve our practices, we invite credible third-party agencies to audit our processes and student achievements. These periodic external assessments allow us to maintain total transparency and make data-driven course corrections.

Our children performed on par with, or better than, India's top CBSE schools in independent assessments by Educational Initiatives (EI–India), CfBT Trust – UK and DFID, UK.

Assessments also show significant improvement in student outcomes at AMC schools following Gyan Shala's intervention.

National Parity: Scoring in the top brackets of elite private schools.

Amisha Parmar

Medical Student (MBBS)

Amisha sets an exemplary example that no obstacle is too big when pulled by your dreams. Scoring an incredible 98.97 percentile in her 12th Grade Science Boards, she is now pursuing her lifelong dream of becoming a doctor.

Living in the Gupta Nagar slums of Ahmedabad, Amisha’s father was the sole breadwinner for a family of six. The financial strain was immense, but the presence of a Gyan Shala center in her vicinity provided her the opportunity to kick-start her journey. She studied with us from Grade 1 through Grade 10, achieving a 97.94 percentile in her 10th Board exams.

Her parents recall with pride: "We heartily accolade Gyan Shala’s profound impact on our girl and its efforts to germinate the seeds of education and build a foundation stone for Amisha’s career." Amisha’s dogged spirit and perseverance continue to inspire her younger siblings and the entire Gyan Shala community.

Meena Parmar

Grade 10 Graduate

Meena Parmar, from Khodiyanagar, Ahmedabad, is a prime example of fighting all odds with a never-give-up attitude. At age 8, Meena's family faced a tragedy when her father passed away, leaving her mother to support seven siblings through field labor. The abject poverty reached a stage where survival was the only goal, and her brothers had to polish shoes to earn a few cents for meals.

During this dark period, Gyan Shala motivated Meena to stay in school. With her dogged spirit and the holistic platform provided by our teachers, she successfully passed her 10th Board exams—the only one among her seven siblings to study that far.

"I want to give special thanks to Gyan Shala and my teachers who always guided me in the right direction and played a role of parents. Their support was beyond a teacher’s role. I pledge to support such organizations that help children like us unleash our potentials."

Chanda sisters

Can you imagine the struggle of parents to fund the education of two daughters, who even face difficulties in managing the daily chores and two-time meals?

This story is a strong case of real struggle and challenges of life. Jinkal (elder) and Kinjal (younger) are two sisters living with their parents in Pragati Nagar slums of Ahmedabad. In order to support livelihood, their parents have struggled a lot. Their father had no option other than to work as traffic police even though his health was not supporting him while mother was working as a field labourer. If this was not enough, they faced extreme conditions when their father suffered from brain damage. Against all odds, Jinkal (elder daughter) recently passed grade 12 examination with 90 percentile while Kinjal (younger daughter) passed grade 10 examination with 89 percentile.

On being interviewed, they shared

"We are blessed to have Gyan Shala platform which helped us to continue our education even though in sheer struggles. We will continue with our studies to stand still in the future so that we could alleviate the pain of our parents and could improve our financial condition."

Tulsi Meghwal

Computer Engineering Student

Tulsi Khetaji Meghwal, a bright student and a daughter of a vegetable vendor, studied until Grade 7 under Gyan Shala’s guidance in the Yogeshwarnagar slum area of Ahmedabad. One of our brightest minds, she achieved a distinction (89%) in her 12th Grade Science stream and is now successfully pursuing her degree in Computer Engineering.

She aims to pursue a Master’s degree in the future and remains a beacon of hope for her community. Reflecting on her journey, Tulsi shares:

"Every child should be made to undergo education like me. What quality education enables a child to do cannot be easily measured. With basic education and inner motivation, it can work wonders for an underprivileged child. Gyan Shala’s continuous support has helped me immensely in believing in myself and moving forward in life."

Tulsi is also committed to ensuring her younger brother follows in her footsteps, advocating for the power of quality education to break the cycle of poverty.

Arun Dantani

Aspirant Engineer

Arun Lalabhai Dantani, a 15-year-old living in the Guptanagar slums of Ahmedabad, is a shining example of dedication. Arun’s parents are vegetable vendors, and the family often struggled to meet basic needs.

He joined Gyan Shala in Grade 1 with no knowledge of alphabets or numbers. Ten years later, his daily routine is a testament to his drive: waking at 5 AM to study, attending Gyan Shala until 1:30 PM, and then working in a wheat-grinding shop from 3:30 PM to 9:30 PM to support his family.

"Now I can read, write; recite poems in Gujarati and English as well. I never thought of dropping from school despite a hectic schedule due to my parent’s support along with Gyan Shala’s able guidance."

Arun secured a distinction with 74% in his 10th Grade exams (2018). His favorite subjects are English, Maths, and Science, and he is now pursuing a diploma in engineering to fulfill his dream of becoming an engineer.

Mahesh Vaghela

Entrepreneur

Mahesh Vinodbhai Vaghela (age 20) is a rising star of Gyan Shala whose journey was tested early. When he was just 15, his father—the family's sole earner—passed away, thrusting Mahesh into the role of primary provider.

To support his mother and two siblings, Mahesh began working as a vegetable vendor, waking at 4:30 AM every day. While these constraints prevented him from pursuing higher education immediately, he didn't let his dreams fade.

"After that incident, I grew stronger. Due to financial and time constraints, I could not pursue higher education. But with renewed vigor in the form of my brother, I will make sure to fulfill his dreams of achieving higher education."

Mahesh credits Gyan Shala with building his faith and understanding, and he now focuses his energy on ensuring his brother (currently in Grade 9 at Gyan Shala) reaches the academic heights he once envisioned for himself.

Dipak Sargara

B.Com Student

Dipak Sargara (age 18), the son of a businessman, is a prime example of the self-reliance Gyan Shala instills in its students. His resilience was put to the test when a severe injury left him bed-ridden for four months during his critical 10th Grade year.

Despite missing months of instruction, the motivation from Gyan Shala helped him pass with flying colors. Since Grade 11, Dipak has been entirely self-reliant, paying his own school and tuition fees.

"With Gyan Shala’s support, I have achieved a good level of self-confidence. I have been working on Computer Accounting for the past three years and am bearing my own education expenses. Gyan Shala is home to me."

Now in his first year of B.Com, Dipak earns around 10,000 INR per month through computer accounting and dreams of supporting the education of other children in low-income communities.
