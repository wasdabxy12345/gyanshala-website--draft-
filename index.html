Our Roots

Gyan Shala was founded by Prof. Pankaj Jain and a team of academic colleagues from IIMA and IRMA. Driven by the mission to provide "elite-school" quality education at a fraction of the cost, they established a model that bridges the learning gap for children in India's most marginalized urban slum communities.

The Gyan Shala Model

Our unique pedagogy uses daily worksheets and a highly structured team-based teaching approach. This system ensures high accountability, rigorous periodic assessments, and learning outcomes that consistently match or exceed those of top private schools.

Vision, Mission & Goal

What drives us, guides us, and defines our purpose

Our Vision

An India where everyone has access to high quality education, achieving 100% literacy regardless of identity or circumstance through universal and easy accessibility.

Our Mission

To develop a high-quality pedagogical model that matches premium standards at a fraction of the cost, and is scalable nationwide without compromising student outcomes.

Our Goal

To bridge the educational divide in India by ensuring children in urban slums and rural villages receive the same caliber of learning as the nation's most privileged children.

Our Valued Donors

Tulsi Meghwal

Computer Engineering Student

Tulsi Khetaji Meghwal, a bright student and a daughter of a vegetable vendor, studied until Grade 7 under Gyan Shala’s guidance in the Yogeshwarnagar slum area of Ahmedabad. One of our brightest minds, she achieved a distinction (89%) in her 12th Grade Science stream and is now successfully pursuing her degree in Computer Engineering.

"Every child should be made to undergo education like me. What quality education enables a child to do cannot be easily measured. With basic education and inner motivation, it can work wonders for an underprivileged child."

Meena Parmar

Grade 10 Graduate

At age 8, Meena's family faced a tragedy when her father passed away, leaving her mother to support seven siblings through field labor. Poverty reached a stage where her brothers had to polish shoes to earn meals.

During this period, Gyan Shala motivated Meena to stay in school. She successfully passed her 10th Board exams—the only one among her seven siblings to study that far.

"I want to give special thanks to Gyan Shala and my teachers who always guided me in the right direction and played a role of parents."

Amisha Parmar

Medical Student (MBBS)

Amisha sets an exemplary example that no obstacle is too big when pulled by your dreams. Scoring an incredible 98.97 percentile in her 12th Grade Science Boards, she is now pursuing her lifelong dream of becoming a doctor.

Living in the Gupta Nagar slums of Ahmedabad, Amisha’s father was the sole breadwinner for a family of six. The financial strain was immense, but the presence of a Gyan Shala center in her vicinity provided her the opportunity to kick-start her journey. She studied with us from Grade 1 through Grade 10, achieving a 97.94 percentile in her 10th Board exams.

Her parents recall with pride: "We heartily accolade Gyan Shala’s profound impact on our girl and its efforts to germinate the seeds of education and build a foundation stone for Amisha’s career." Amisha’s dogged spirit and perseverance continue to inspire her younger siblings and the entire Gyan Shala community.

Chavda sisters

Can you imagine the struggle of parents to fund the education of two daughters, who even face difficulties in managing the daily chores and two-time meals?

Jinkal (elder) and Kinjal (younger) are two sisters living with their parents in Pragati Nagar slums of Ahmedabad. Against all odds, Jinkal recentemente passed grade 12 examination with 90 percentile while Kinjal passed grade 10 examination with 89 percentile.

"We are blessed to have Gyan Shala platform which helped us to continue our education even though in sheer struggles."
